About the Coronavirus

COVID 19 or the Coronavirus

The COVID-19 or the Coronavirus is an infectious disease caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome. It was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, China and has resulted in an ongoing pandemic. As of the end of June 2020, more than 10.3 million cases have been reported across 188 countries and territories resulting in more than 505,000 deaths.

There are no vaccines nor specific anti-viral treatments for COVID-19. Management involves the treatment of symptoms, supportive care, isolation and experimental methods. The World Health Organisation declared the COVID-19 outbreak a public health emergency of international concern.
