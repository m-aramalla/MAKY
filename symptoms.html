Symptoms of the Coronavirus

The most common symptoms of the Coronavirus are:

Fever

A fever is a temparory increase in your body temparature often due to illness. Having a fever is a sign that something out of the ordinary is going on in your body.

Dry cough

A dry cough is a cough that doesn't bring up mucus. It may feel like you have a tickle in the back of your throat triggering your cough giving you hacking coughs.Dry coughs occur because there is inflammation or irritation in your respiratory tract.

Tiredness

Tiredness is the state of wishing for sleep or rest .

Aches and pains

Continuous or prolonged dull pain in a part of one's body.These are minor pains and discomforts typically in the muscles.

Sore throat

A sore throat is pain,scratchiness or irritation of the throat that often worsens when you swallow. The most common cause of a sore throat is a viral infection, such as the cold or the flu.

Diarrhoea

A condition where faeces are discharged from the bowels frequently and in a liquid form.

Conjunctivitis

.Conjunctivitis is an inflammation or infection of the transparent membrane that lines your eyelid and covers the white part of your eyeball.When small blood vessels in the membrane become inflamed, they are more visible. This causes the whites of your eyes to appear reddish pink.

Headache

Headache is defined as a pain arising from the head or upper neck of the body.The pain originates form the tissues and structures that surround the skull or the brain because the brain itself has no nerves that give rise to the sensation of pain.

Loss of taste or smell

Impaired taste and smell means that your sense of taste and smell is not functioning properly.

A rash on skin

A rash is a noticable change in the texture or th colour of your skin.Your skin may become scaly,bumpy,itchy or otherwise irritated.

Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath.

When you are short of breath, its hard or uncomfortable for you to take in oxygen your body needs. You may feel as if you are not getting enough air.

Chest pain or pressure.

Chest pain or discomfort is caused when your heart muscle doesn't get enough oxygen rich blood.It may feel like pressure or squeezing in your chest.

Seek immediate medical attention if you have serious symptoms. Always call before visiting your doctor or health facility.

People with mild symptoms who are otherwise healthy should manage their symptoms at home.

On average it takes 5-6 days from when someone is infected with the virus for symptoms to show, however it can take up to 14 days.

KNOW HOW COVID-19 IS SPREAD

  • You can become infected by coming into close contact(about six feet or two arms length) with a person who has COVID 19. COVID 19 is primarily spread from person to person.
  • You can become infected from respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.
  • You may also be able to get it by touching a surface or object that has the virus on it, and then by touching your mouth, nose, or eyes.

PROTECT YOURSELF AND OTHERS FROM COVID 19

  • There is currently no vaccine to protect against COVID-19. The best way protect yourself is to avoid being exposed to the virus that causes COVID 19.
  • Stay home as much as possible and avoid close contact with others
  • Wear a cloth face covering that covers your nose and mouth in public settings.
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces.
  • Wash your hands often with soap and water for atleast 20 seconds or use an alcohol based hand sanitiser that contains at least 60% alcohol.

PRACTICE SOCIAL DISTANCING

  • Buy groceries and medicines, go to the doctor and complete banking activities online when possible.
  • If you must go in person, stay at least 6 feet away from others and disinfect items you must touch.
  • Get deliveries and takeout and limit in person contact as much as possible.

PREVENT THE SPREAD OF COVID 19 IF YOU ARE SICK

  • Stay home if you are sick except to get medical care.
  • Avoid public transportation, ride-sharing or taxis.
  • Distance yourself from other people and pets in your home.
  • There is no specific treatment for COVID 19, but you can seek medical care to help relieve your symptoms..
  • If you need medical attention, call ahead.

KNOW YOUR RISK FOR SEVERE ILLNESS

  • Everyone is at risk of getting COVID 19.
  • Older adults and people of any age who have serious underlying medical conditions may be at higher risk for severe illness.
